ACADEMIC INTERESTS

  • My Academic Interests lie in Theoretical Computer Science and Mathematics. I am currently thinking about the following problems:
    • The limit of quantum approximate optimization on models from Spin-Glass Theory in Statistical Physics. I am interested in exploring the duality between diluted spin-glass models and random CSPs via the Parisi Variational Principle and the Overlap/RSB structure of these problems, with implications on algorithmic hardness.
    • Possible connections between Wigner Function negativity & Sum-of-Squares pseudodistributions, particularly in the Unique-Games Conjecture regime. Generically, any connections between RS/RSB ansatze and SoS pseudo-distributions would have interesting algorithmic implications.
    • The use of High-Dimensional Expanders in the creation of better Quantum-Error Correction Codes and for provable speed-ups in approximate sampling & optimization algorithms.
  • Broadly, my interests are at the intersection of Spin-Glass Theory, Quantum Inapproximability, Convex Analysis, Optimization and Random/Spectral Graph Theory. I am curious about the average-case complexity landscape of combinatorial optimization problems for large families of (classical & quantum) algorithms. Key tools that I often use in my work are: Stochastic Processes, Probability Theory, Convexity, Spectral Graph Theory, Spin Glass Theory, Quantum Algorithms & Partial Differential Equations.
  • I am fortunate to have some wonderful collaborators. I work very closely on many problems with the wonderful duo of Antares Chen and Jonathan Shi. I have also worked on some exciting projects with Chi-Ning Chou, Emil Khabiboulline and Jonathan Wurtz.

ACADEMIC INTERESTS

  • My Academic Interests mainly lie in the areas of Theoretical Computer Science and Discrete Math. These days, I am thinking about problems in three areas - The first is trying to understand the limit of Quantum Approximate Optimization by formally reasoning about shallow-depth QAOA(p) on a large number of models from Spin-Glass Theory in Statistical Physics. The second involves thinking about connections between Wigner Function negativity & Sum-of-Squares pseudodistributions, particularly in the UGC regime. The third is the use of High-Dimensional Expanders in the creation of better Quantum-Error Correction Codes and for speed-ups in approximate Sampling & Optimization problems.
  • More generally, I have interests in the connections between Statistical Physics and Algorithmic Hardness, in particular to the understand the average-case complexity landscape of combinatorial optimization problems for large families of (classical & quantum) algorithms.
  • Key themes/ideas/tools that show up often in my projects/works/musings include: Stochastic Processes, Overlap Gap Property, Probability Theory, Spectral Graph Theory, Spin Glasses, Quantum Algorithms & Partial Differential Equations.

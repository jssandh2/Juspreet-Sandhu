ACADEMIC INTERESTS

  • My Academic Interests mainly lie in the (broad) area of Theoretical Computer Science and Discrete Math. Currently, I am thinking about problems in three areas. The first is trying to understand the limit of Quantum Approximation Optimization by formally reasoning about shallow-depth QAOA(p) on a large number of models from Spin-Glass Theory in Statistical Physics. The second involves thinking about connections between Wigner Function negativity & Sum-of-Squares pseudodistributions, particularly in the UGC regime. The third is the use of High-Dimensional Expanders in the creation of better Quantum-Error Correction Codes and, more generally, the use of High-Dimensional Expanders for problems in Sampling & Optimization.
  • More generally, I have broad interests in:
    • Quantum Hamiltonian Complexity & Adiabatic Quantum Complexity/QAOA
    • Quantum Games & Communication Complexity
    • Spectral & Random Graph Theory, Matrix Analysis & Discrete Fourier Analysis
    • Probabilistic & Additive Combinatorics in Pseudorandomness
